Dra'Cora

A living D&D campaign world built around consequence, player agency, and the slow collapse of a world that was never supposed to exist.

Year 2026
Role Narrative Designer, World Designer
Status Ongoing
Tools Inkarnate, Notion
The World
A world built on the bones of a dead god

Dra'Cora is a D&D campaign world I have been designing and expanding as a personal narrative project. The world itself is built on an uncomfortable truth: the land, the sky, and everything living in it exists because a creator deity was killed, and the remnants of that being became the foundation for everything that came after.

Players step into this world without knowing its history. They are ordinary people drawn into something much larger than themselves, connected to an ancient seal they did not ask to be part of. As the campaign unfolds, the choices they make shape not just what happens to them, but what happens to the world around them.

The map is made in Inkarnate and is deliberately kept as a living document. Names change, regions shift, and the landscape itself responds to what players have done. Old settlements disappear. New ones appear. The map is never finished because the world is never finished.

Core Conflict
The False Dragon King

The central conflict of Dra'Cora revolves around the potential return of the False Dragon King, a being born from what was left of the slain goddess. After the world's creation, his essence was fractured and scattered across the land in the form of relics.

For a long time, those relics stayed dormant. But something has changed. They are surfacing again, and wherever they appear, factions move to claim them: to use them, destroy them, or study them. The problem is that every time a relic is activated, reality around it destabilises slightly. Use enough of them and the world begins to unravel.

Players become part of this without anyone asking them if they want to be. Their connection to the ancient seal means they are always in proximity to these events, always affected by them, and always making choices that ripple outward in ways they cannot fully predict.

Narrative Mechanics
Systems that shape the story
System 01
Relic System

Each relic carries a fragment of the False King's power. They grant significant abilities while quietly influencing the world around whoever holds them. Players must weigh the benefit of using a relic against what using it costs the world.

System 02
Corruption and Transformation

Prolonged exposure to certain forces changes characters over time. These changes show up both in how the story develops and in the mechanics themselves: altered behaviour, shifting perception, and abilities that were not there before.

System 03
Sha'vith

An external force that operates beyond anything the inhabitants of Dra'Cora can understand. It does not fight directly. It works through altered perception, identity distortion, and a slow erosion of who a person believes they are.

Factions and Forces
No one agrees on what should be done
Revivalist Cults
Groups working to bring the False Dragon King back. They see his return as the restoration of something that was taken from the world, not as a threat to it.
Scholarly Orders
Researchers trying to understand relic power before someone else uses it catastrophically. They believe knowledge and control are the only way forward.
Political Powers
Institutions that see the chaos caused by surfacing relics as an opportunity. They do not care about the False King either way, only about consolidating influence while everyone else is distracted.
Unknown External Entities
Forces that operate at the edges of comprehension. Players may encounter their influence long before they understand what is behind it, or whether it even has a goal they could recognise.
Tone and Themes
High fantasy with a psychological edge

Dra'Cora blends high fantasy setting with psychological tension. Victories in this world are rarely clean. Consequences are rarely immediate. The story does not reward players for doing the heroic thing; it asks them to figure out what the heroic thing even is when there are no clear villains, just competing beliefs.

Design Philosophy
Built to support many campaigns

The systems in Dra'Cora are designed to carry multiple narrative experiences. The core mechanics around relic influence and corruption stay consistent across campaigns, but how they express themselves changes depending on where in the world the story takes place, which factions are involved, and what players have already done.

This means each campaign can feel like a distinct story while still taking place in the same living world. Player decisions from one campaign can leave marks that the next group discovers without anyone explaining what happened.

What comes next

Dra'Cora is the foundation for a series of campaigns, each exploring different regions, conflicts, and player stories within the same world.
